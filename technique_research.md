# Prompt Engineering Technique: Feynman Learning
## Description

```
Explain concepts simply, identify knowledge gaps, then deepen understanding
```
The Feynman technique is a 4 step mental model for understanding and learning a wide variety of topics. The first step is `choosing a concept`, then `explaining it as if teaching a child`, then `finding gaps in your understanding`, and finally `refining the explanation`. If the explanation is not satisfactory, repeat steps 3 and 4 until a satisfactory and complete explanation is reached.

This technique, developed by physicist Richard Feynman, is a way to transform information into knowledge. 

Often in a school environment, topics are given definitions containing wordy jargon that a child would not be able to understand, so the act of boiling these complex ideas down into a digestible format with words a child can understand proves an understanding of the underlying meanings and concepts rather than simple rote memorization.

Identifying knowledge gaps is also powerful, as it is an example of `metacognition` ("thinking about thinking"), which can prevent illusions of competence or thinking you know more than you actually do.

>If you can't explain it to a six-year-old, you don't understand it yourself - Albert Einstein

## Justification For Selection
Feynman Learning appealed to me because I believe it offers the most optimal and intuitive way to get a good understanding of various topics. I sometimes find myself unable to accurately and satisfyingly explain the topics I am currently learning or have learned in the past, and utilizing the Feynman technique can fill in the gaps of my understanding, which I either never knew or just forgot. We often think we know more than we actually do, and the Feynman technique is a good way to expose the holes in our understanding.

## Applications of Feynman Learning
The Feynman technique is an excellent way to strengthen understanding in conceptual and theoretical topics. Subjects like mathematics, physics, computer science, philosophy, and history all fall into this category, especially if one needs to explain these topics clearly. While being able to explain these topics in simple words is technically the goal of Feynman Learning, the process of achieving that goal is the real purpose, as it is what strengthens one's understanding.

## The Role of AI
Feynman Learning and AI, more specifically a powerful chatbot, complement each other greatly as AI can take the role of a student or a teacher and ask questions to further challenge one's understanding of a subject, while actually providing explanations if needed. AI has the power of being able to act in various roles while being relatively unbiased (depending on the biases of those training the AI), so it can seamlessly switch roles based on the needs of the user.
